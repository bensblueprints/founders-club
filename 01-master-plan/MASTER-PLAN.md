# Master Plan — FOUNDERS Meetups (Da Nang Pilot)

_Last updated: 2026-07-09_

## 1. The idea in one paragraph
A paid, curated networking dinner for founders in Da Nang. We run targeted ads → founders land on an event page → they pay $150 → they attend a capped (25-person) dinner. The differentiator is a **digital layer**: an app/web app where every attendee can browse everyone else's profile (what they do, what they're building, what they're looking for) so connections happen intentionally instead of through random small talk.

## 2. Why this can work (and the honest risks)
**Working for us**
- **Validated format** — Benji's team has run this style of founder dinner before; it worked.
- Expert ad targeting in-house (Benji) → cheaper, sharper reach.
- Strong technical team (An + Benji) → we can build the digital layer ourselves, no vendor cost.
- Small, capped format → low logistics risk, easy to make feel premium.
- Da Nang has a real, concentrated founder / digital-nomad / remote-founder scene.

**Learnings from Benji's prior event (build these in)**
- **Language split:** ~50% Vietnamese / 50% international, and many VN attendees don't speak English. → **The host/MC must be bilingual (VI + EN)**, and key materials should be bilingual. This is a hard requirement, not a nice-to-have.
- **Open question — prior pricing:** the anecdote mentions "100,000" (VND?) and separately "$100" for the taken *Founder Vietnam* event. Clarify what was actually charged before so we set the $150 expectation correctly.

**Risks to watch**
- **Cold audience.** We have no existing founder network. Every one of the first 25 must come from ads → trust is the bottleneck at a $150 price with an unknown brand.
- **Filling the room on a lean budget.** Ad spend is capped at $20–30, so cost isn't the risk — *reach* is. $150 to an unknown event is a high bar, and a tiny budget must lean on targeting + organic + seeding to sell out. See [Budget](../06-budget-and-finance/BUDGET.md).
- **Chicken-and-egg.** People pay to meet "great founders," but early on we can't guarantee who's in the room. → We may need to seed the room (see §6).
- **Time.** Late-July target is ~3 weeks out. Aggressive but doable for a 25-cap pilot if we lock decisions tonight.

## 6. Key strategic decisions to make (flag for the call)
1. **Do we seed the room?** Consider hand-inviting 5–8 credible founders for free/discounted to guarantee quality for the first 25. Ads then sell the remaining seats. This de-risks the "who's in the room" problem.
2. **Refund / guarantee policy.** At $150 cold, a "money-back if you don't make a valuable connection" or "full refund if we cancel / don't hit min headcount" reduces buyer hesitation.
3. **Minimum viable headcount.** What's the fewest paid tickets at which we still run vs. postpone? (Suggest ~12–15.)
4. **Price test.** Is $150 right for a first, unproven event in Da Nang? Consider an early-bird ($99) to build momentum, then $150.
5. **Positioning.** Who exactly is "a founder" here? (Startup founders, agency owners, indie hackers, e-commerce, nomad entrepreneurs?) This drives ad targeting AND who feels the room is "for them."
